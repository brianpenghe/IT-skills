# Skill: Enable Night Shift on macOS for Eye Comfort and Better Sleep

## Purpose

Reduce eye strain and minimize blue light exposure during the evening by
enabling **Night Shift** on macOS. This is especially helpful when
working for long periods on external monitors.

## When to Use

-   Working after sunset.
-   Reading papers or coding for extended periods.
-   Using external monitors with a MacBook in clamshell (lid-closed)
    mode.
-   Reducing blue light before bedtime to support healthier sleep.

## Steps

1.  Open **System Settings**.
2.  Select **Displays**.
3.  Click **Night Shift**.
4.  Configure the schedule:
    -   **Sunset to Sunrise** (recommended), or
    -   A custom schedule if preferred.
5.  Adjust the **Warmth** slider to a comfortable level.
    -   Warmer settings reduce blue light more aggressively.
    -   Choose the strongest warmth that still allows comfortable
        reading.

## Notes

-   Night Shift changes the display's color temperature according to the
    time of day.
-   It continues to function when using external monitors in most
    configurations.
-   Unlike **True Tone**, Night Shift does **not** rely on the Mac's
    ambient light sensor, making it suitable for clamshell mode.
-   Automatic ambient-light-based brightness adjustment is not available
    for external monitors through macOS when the MacBook lid is closed.

## Best Practices

-   Enable **Night Shift** with the **Sunset to Sunrise** schedule.
-   Combine Night Shift with **Dark Mode** in the evening.
-   Lower monitor brightness during nighttime for additional eye
    comfort.
-   Take regular breaks following the **20-20-20 rule**:
    -   Every 20 minutes,
    -   Look at something about 20 feet (6 meters) away,
    -   For at least 20 seconds.

## Outcome

Night Shift provides an effortless, built-in way to reduce blue light
exposure in the evening, making prolonged screen use more comfortable
and helping prepare the body for sleep without requiring additional
software.
